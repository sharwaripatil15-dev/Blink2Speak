Blink2Speak – User Guide

Learn how to communicate using eye blinks and Morse-style patterns.

🔹 What is Blink2Speak?

Blink2Speak is an assistive communication system for people who cannot speak or move freely. It detects eye blinks and converts Morse-style patterns into text and spoken messages.

The goal is to let you express basic needs, feelings, and sentences with just your eyes, so you can interact with caregivers, family, and medical staff more comfortably.

🟦 How to Use the App

  • 1. Open the Live Camera or Communicate page.
  • 2. Position your face clearly in front of the camera.
  • 3. Blink naturally to send short signals (•) and long blinks (—).
  • 4. The system automatically detects and converts your blink pattern into characters.
  • 5. Completed letters form words, which appear in the output box.
  • 6. Use quick phrases (Yes, No, Help, I need water, etc.) for faster responses.
  • 7. Activate speech output if you want the app to read your message aloud.

🟦 Blink Meaning (Morse Style)

  • • Short Blink = Dot (•)
  • — Long Blink = Dash (—)
  • Short pause = End of a letter
  • Longer pause = Space between words
  • Very long pause = End of sentence

You do not need to memorize the entire Morse code table on day one. Start with a few letters such as E (•) and T (—), then gradually learn more combinations like “YES” and “NO”.

🟩 Practice Mode (Recommended)

Before using Blink2Speak for real conversations, spend a few minutes practicing. This helps the system calibrate to your blink speed and reduces errors.

  • • Practice making clear short blinks and long blinks.
  • • Try spelling simple words such as “HI”, “YES”, or “NO”.
  • • Ask a caregiver to check if the decoded text matches your intention.
  • • Adjust sensitivity and minimum blink duration in the Settings page if needed.

🟦 Tips for Best Detection

  • • Ensure good lighting so your eyes are clearly visible.
  • • Sit directly facing the camera at eye level.
  • • Keep the camera steady and avoid shaking the device.
  • • Avoid extremely slow or half‑closed blinks, which can confuse detection.
  • • If you wear glasses, clean the lenses to reduce reflections.

If the app stops recognizing blinks correctly, pause for a moment, correct your position, and resume detection from the main camera screen.

🟧 Comfort & Safety Notes

  • • Take regular breaks to avoid eye strain.
  • • Stop using the app if your eyes feel dry, painful, or irritated.
  • • Use artificial tears if recommended by your doctor for long sessions.
  • • Ask caregivers to adjust the screen distance so you can see comfortably.

Blink2Speak is a support tool and does not replace medical advice. Always consult healthcare professionals for any changes in your condition.

🟦 Quick Phrases, History & Saved Messages

  • • Use quick phrase buttons to say common messages in one tap.
  • • Open the History page to review what you communicated earlier.
  • • Save important messages (for example, “I am in pain”) for instant reuse.
  • • Caregivers can pre‑save helpful phrases in your own language.

🛠️ Recommended Settings Overview

If Blink2Speak feels too sensitive or not sensitive enough, adjust these options in the Settings page:

  • • Blink detection – turn on/off real‑time eye tracking.
  • • Sensitivity – higher values detect smaller or faster blinks.
  • • Minimum blink duration – controls how the app separates dots and dashes.
  • • Sound on blink – play a soft click whenever a blink is detected.
  • • Voice volume & style – change how the spoken output sounds.

💚 Getting the Most From Blink2Speak

Learning a new communication method takes time. Start slow, use practice mode, and rely on quick phrases while you become more confident with Morse patterns.

Share this guide with your family and caregivers so everyone understands how Blink2Speak works and can support you in daily communication.
